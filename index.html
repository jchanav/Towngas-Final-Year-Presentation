Section 2 — Proposed Solution

Our Solution: 7-Day Rolling Horizon MILP

What We Propose

  • MILP jointly optimises all 4 daily decisions (CT collection, NP/TM transfers, EX offload/retrieval)
  • 7-day lookahead — only day-1 decisions are executed; the horizon rolls forward each morning
  • Decision-support tool — manager reviews and approves all recommendations

Why This Helps Towngas

  • Optimises total weekly cost, not just today's decision in isolation
  • Consistent and auditable — every recommendation has a clear cost rationale
  • Complements expertise — model handles constraints and costs; manager adds domain knowledge

Section 2 — Method Justification

Why MILP with Rolling Horizon?

Method Optimality Data Requirement Interpretability Runtime Fit for This Problem
MILP + Rolling Horizon Provable Confirmed orders + costs High Seconds per day ✓ Our choice
Stochastic Programming Scenario-based Demand distributions Low Hours Adds complexity; demand largely known here
Reinforcement Learning Policy-based Large training set Low High (training) Black-box; hard to justify to managers

Deterministic Approach is Appropriate

  • Project and prepick orders are confirmed days in advance — low demand uncertainty
  • Stochastic programming adds complexity without clear benefit when most demand is known
  • Moving average handles residual uncertainty outside the optimisation

Provable Optimality & Auditability

  • Gurobi solver certifies solution quality with a guaranteed optimality gap
  • Every daily recommendation has a quantified cost rationale
  • Essential for managerial trust and operational accountability

Rolling Horizon Handles Changing Conditions

  • New containers arrive daily; order books update throughout the week
  • Re-optimises each morning with fresh data, naturally correcting for forecast errors
  • Only day-1 decisions are committed — future plans adapt as new information arrives

Section 3 — Mathematical Formulation

Full Theoretical Model: Formulation

Sets & Indices

  • $\mathcal{I}$ — set of SKUs
  • $\mathcal{W} = \{\mathrm{NP,TM}\}$ — internal warehouses
  • $\mathcal{C}$ — containers at the Cargo Terminal
  • $\mathcal{T} = \{1,\ldots,H\}$ — planning horizon ($H=7$ days)
  • $\mathcal{H} \subseteq \mathcal{T}$ — holidays (warehouse closed)
  • $\mathcal{W}^{+} = \mathcal{T}\setminus\mathcal{H}$ — working days
  • $\mathcal{P}$ — active project/prepick order lines

Key Decision Variables

  • $\delta_{c,w,t} \in \{0,1\}$ — collect container $c$ to warehouse $w$ on day $t$
  • $y^{w\to w'}_{i,t} \geq 0$ — transfer SKU $i$ from $w$ to $w'$
  • $x^{\mathrm{EX}\to w}_{i,t},\; x^{w\to\mathrm{EX}}_{i,t} \geq 0$ — EX retrieve / offload
  • $s_{i,w,t} \geq 0$ — walk-in lost sales; $\;B_{i,w,t} \geq 0$ — unfulfilled order carryover
  • $I_{i,w,t} \in \mathbb{R}$ — signed inventory position; $\;\eta_{c,t} \in [0,1]$ — CT overstay indicator

Objective Function

$$ \min \sum_{t \in \mathcal{T}} \gamma^{t-1}\!\Bigl[ \underbrace{h^{\mathrm{EX}}_d \!\sum_i v_i I^{\mathrm{EX}}_{i,t}}_{\text{(A) EX holding}} + \underbrace{h^{\mathrm{EX}}_a \!\sum_i v_i x^{w\to\mathrm{EX}}_{i,t}}_{\text{(B) EX arrival}} + \underbrace{\sum_w R^{\mathrm{EX}\to w}_t}_{\text{(C) EX retrieval}} + \underbrace{\sum_w R^{w\to\mathrm{EX}}_t}_{\text{(D) EX offload}} $$ $$ + \underbrace{\sum_{i,w} p_i s_{i,w,t}}_{\text{(E) Lost sales}} + \underbrace{\sum_{i,w} b_i B_{i,w,t}}_{\text{(F) Unfulfilled orders}} + \underbrace{\sum_{i,w} \omega^w v_i \delta^+_{i,w,t}}_{\text{(G) Space opp.}} + \underbrace{\sum_{c,w} F^{\mathrm{CT}}_{|V_c|} \delta_{c,w,t}}_{\text{(I) CT transport}} + \underbrace{\sum_c h^{\mathrm{CT}} V_c \eta_{c,t}}_{\text{(J) CT overstay}} \Bigr]$$ $$+ \gamma^{H-1}\underbrace{\sum_{i,w}\bigl[\gamma^- \delta^{-,H}_{i,w} + \gamma^+ \omega^w v_i \delta^{+,H}_{i,w}\bigr]}_{\text{(H) Terminal value}}$$

Discount Factor $\gamma^{t-1}$

  • Weights day-1 costs at full value; progressively discounts future days
  • Reflects decreasing forecast reliability as the horizon extends
  • Future decisions will be revised as the horizon rolls forward each day

Section 3 — Mathematical Formulation

Full Theoretical Model: Key Constraints

Inventory Balance

Tracks goods in = goods out at each warehouse every day

$$I_{i,w,t} = I_{i,w,t-1} - B_{i,w,t-1} + r_{i,w,t} + \mathrm{CT}_{i,w,t} + y^{w'\!\to w}_{i,t} - y^{w\to w'}_{i,t} + x^{\mathrm{EX}\!\to w}_{i,t} - x^{w\to\mathrm{EX}}_{i,t} - D^{\mathrm{det}}_{i,w,t} - D^{\mathrm{sto}}_{i,w,t} + u_{i,w,t} + s_{i,w,t}$$

Warehouse Capacity

Total volume in each warehouse cannot exceed its floor space

$$\sum_i v_i \max(0, I_{i,w,t}) \leq K^w \quad \forall w,\; t$$

Internal Transfer Capacity

Daily truck trips limit how much can move between NP and TM

$$\sum_i \frac{v_i}{V_P} y^{w\to w'}_{i,t} \leq n^{\mathrm{trips}} \cdot P^{\mathrm{trip}} \quad \forall t \in \mathcal{W}^+$$

CT Collection Rules

Each container collected at most once; not before it arrives at CT

$$\sum_w \sum_{t} \delta_{c,w,t} \leq 1 \quad \forall c; \qquad \delta_{c,w,t} = 0 \text{ if } t < \tau_c$$

CT Intake Capacity

Limits how many containers each warehouse can receive per day

$$\sum_{i,c} v_i\, a_{i,c}\, \delta_{c,w,t} \leq P^{\mathrm{intake}}_w \quad \forall w,\; t \in \mathcal{W}^+$$

CT Overstay Indicator

Flags containers left at CT past their free storage period — triggers penalty

$$\eta_{c,t} \leq 1 - \sum_w \sum_{\tau \leq t} \delta_{c,w,\tau}, \quad t \geq \bar{\tau}^{\mathrm{FSP}}_c$$

Why Greedy?

  • Mimics how a busy manager reacts to today's needs
  • Collects containers when urgency arises (demand or FSP)
  • Represents a reasonable "human heuristic" baseline

Why Rule-Based?

  • Simple policy: always collect containers on arrival day
  • Common in practice — feels safe and predictable
  • Ignores warehouse space readiness, may inflate EX costs

Why Actual Operations?

  • Reconstructed from Towngas's own SAP data
  • Shows the real cost of current practice
  • Most operationally meaningful comparison

Why External Warehouse Cost Is the True Performance Measure

CT transport cost is unavoidable — containers must eventually be collected regardless of the model used, and only the timing differs. External warehouse (EX) costs, by contrast, are directly determined by decision quality and are wholly avoidable through better planning. Since all four models achieve zero backlog and zero CT overstay, minimising EX-related expenditure is the primary dimension on which model performance should be assessed.

Section 6 — Limitations & Future Work

Current Limitations & Further Developments

Data
Scope
Planning
Prototype
Integration
Limitations
L1

CT Arrival Date is Estimated

Arrival date $\tau_c$ is inferred from goods receipt records — digital invoice dates were unavailable, so simulation uses an estimated proxy.

↳ Improvement
  • Run with real data: verified invoice dates yield more robust benchmark results
L2

APPL-Only, Incomplete Capacity Picture

APPL shares NP/TM space with spare parts and white goods. Without full CBM data for other categories, capacity constraints are estimated and may not reflect true utilisation.

↳ Improvement
  • Expand stock scope: add CBM data for spare parts and white goods in NP/TM
L3

7-Day Rolling Horizon

A 7-day lookahead may miss longer-term demand patterns, procurement lead times, and seasonal peaks.

↳ Improvement
  • Extend horizon: test 14-day lookahead for better forward demand anticipation
  • Trade-off analysis: benchmark quality gain vs. solve time at 14 days
Further Developments
F1

Hardcoded Cost & Capacity Parameters

Cost parameters and capacity limits are hardcoded — not adjustable through the Streamlit interface.

↳ Development
  • Interactive parameter panel: configure cost rates, capacity limits, and horizon length at runtime
F2

Manual SAP/ERP Data Preparation

Input format does not perfectly match raw SAP exports — manual data cleaning is required before each upload.

↳ Development
  • Automated pipeline: read and clean directly from Towngas SAP exports
  • Built-in validation: data-quality checks to guide users through upload
